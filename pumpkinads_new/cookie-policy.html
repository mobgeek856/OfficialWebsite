Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how PUMPKIN TECHNOLOGY (“we,” “our,” or “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website or use our online services. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to understand how visitors use the site. We may also use similar technologies such as pixel tags, local storage, or SDK-based identifiers in our advertising technology.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Strictly necessary: Required for the website to function (e.g., security, load balancing, session management).
  • Functional: To remember your choices and preferences (e.g., language, region) and to provide enhanced features.
  • Analytics and performance: To understand how visitors use our website, which pages are most popular, and how we can improve the user experience.
  • Advertising and marketing: In the context of our ad tech services, we or our partners may use cookies and identifiers to deliver and measure advertising across websites and apps, in accordance with our agreements and applicable law.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Our website or services may include content or integrations from third parties (e.g., analytics providers, advertising partners). These third parties may set their own cookies or similar technologies when you interact with our site. We do not control these cookies. Please refer to the respective third parties’ privacy and cookie policies for more information.

5. Your Choices

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. You can usually find these options in your browser’s “Settings” or “Preferences” menu. Blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of our website.
  • Consent: Where required by law, we will obtain your consent before placing non-essential cookies. You can withdraw consent or update your preferences at any time through our cookie preference center (if available) or by contacting us.

6. Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control

Some browsers offer a “Do Not Track” (DNT) or similar signal. There is no universal standard for how websites must respond to DNT. We currently do not change our practices based on DNT signals alone, but we respect your choices made through cookie settings and consent. We may support Global Privacy Control (GPC) or similar signals where technically and legally required.

7. Updates

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. We will post the revised policy on this page and update the “Last updated” date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
